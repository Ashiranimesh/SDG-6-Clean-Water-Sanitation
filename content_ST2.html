🌍 Why It Matters

Water covers 71% of our planet, yet only 3% is freshwater — and less than 1% is accessible to us. According to the United Nations, over 2 billion people currently live in countries experiencing high water stress, and this number is rising every year (UN, 2023).

2.2B People lack safe drinking water globally (WHO/UNICEF, 2021)
140L Average daily water use per person in the UK (Environment Agency, 2022)
~30% Of home water use wasted through leaks and inefficient habits (EPA, 2023)

SDG 6 — Clean Water and Sanitation — calls on every individual, community, and institution to take responsibility for how water is used and protected. The home is the most immediate place where real change can start. Changing a handful of daily habits can reduce household water consumption by up to 50%, saving money and safeguarding the future of this irreplaceable resource.

Did you know? A single dripping tap can waste more than 5,500 litres of water per year — enough to fill a small paddling pool more than 70 times (Environment Agency, 2022).

🍽️ Kitchen & Cooking

The kitchen is one of the highest-consumption areas in the home. Simple behavioural changes can dramatically reduce waste without sacrificing convenience.

Top Kitchen Actions

  • Only run full dishwasher loads. A full dishwasher uses around 9–12 litres per cycle, compared to up to 60 litres for hand-washing the same dishes under a running tap.
  • Wash fruits and vegetables in a bowl, not under a running tap. Re-use the collected water to irrigate houseplants.
  • Keep a jug of drinking water in the fridge so you do not have to run the tap waiting for it to cool.
  • Defrost food in the fridge overnight rather than under running water.
  • Use the minimum water needed when boiling vegetables — it also preserves more nutrients.
  • Check and fix dripping taps promptly. A slow drip can add up to thousands of litres wasted each year.
Quick win: Switching from hand-washing to a full dishwasher load just once per day saves an estimated 18,000 litres of water annually for an average UK household (Energy Saving Trust, 2023).

🚿 Bathroom Habits

The bathroom accounts for around 70% of a household's total indoor water use. Showers, baths, and toilet flushes are the biggest contributors — and the easiest to address.

Shower Smarter

A standard shower uses approximately 8–10 litres of water per minute. Cutting just two minutes from your shower saves around 16–20 litres every time. Multiply that across a household of four over a year, and the savings are remarkable.

  • Aim for showers under 4 minutes — use a shower timer or a favourite short song as a guide.
  • Install a water-efficient showerhead (low-flow) to reduce flow without sacrificing pressure.
  • Collect cold water while waiting for the shower to warm up — use it to water plants or flush the toilet.
  • Choose showers over baths: a full bath uses 80–150 litres, versus 32–80 litres for a 4-minute shower.

Toilet & Sink

  • Use the half-flush option on dual-flush toilets for liquid waste (uses ~3 litres vs 6 litres for a full flush).
  • Never use the toilet as a bin — each unnecessary flush wastes 6–9 litres.
  • Turn the tap off while brushing teeth — a running tap uses 6 litres per minute.
  • Fix a leaking toilet cistern: a slow internal leak wastes up to 400 litres per day silently (WaterWise, 2022).
  • Install a tap aerator to reduce flow rate without reducing water pressure.
Impact reminder: Turning off the tap while brushing teeth twice a day saves up to 12 litres daily — that is over 4,000 litres every year, per person.

🌱 Garden & Outdoor

Outdoor water use can represent up to 40% of household consumption during summer months, largely through garden watering and car washing (Environment Agency, 2022). Smart outdoor habits protect water during the seasons when demand is highest.

Smart Gardening

  • Install a water butt to harvest rainwater from gutters — completely free, naturally soft, and better for plants than treated tap water.
  • Water plants in the early morning or evening to minimise evaporation losses during the heat of the day.
  • Use a watering can instead of a hosepipe — hosepipes use approximately 1,000 litres per hour; a watering can uses around 10 litres.
  • Choose drought-tolerant native plants that are adapted to local rainfall patterns and require minimal supplemental watering.
  • Apply a 5–8 cm mulch layer around plants to reduce surface evaporation and retain soil moisture for longer.

Washing Cars & Outdoor Surfaces

  • Wash the car using a bucket (typically 10–15 litres) rather than a hosepipe (1,000 litres/hr).
  • Use a stiff brush to sweep driveways and paths instead of hosing them down.
  • Consider waterless car-wash products for quick cleaning between full washes.
Summer fact: During a dry UK summer, hosepipe bans can be imposed when reservoirs fall below critical levels. Building good outdoor water-saving habits year-round prevents shortages before they reach crisis point.

🌏 From Home to Global Impact

Individual actions might feel small, but when adopted collectively across communities, cities, and countries, they generate profound systemic change.

Imagine every household in the UK saving just 20 litres of water per day through the simple habits described on this page. With approximately 28 million households, that equates to 560 million litres saved daily — enough to supply the drinking-water needs of an entire small country for a week.

Water conservation at home also reduces the energy required to pump, heat, and treat water, cutting household carbon emissions. Every saved litre supports the infrastructure that delivers clean water to those who currently lack it, freeing up capacity in overstretched water systems around the world.

SDG 6 is not just a policy target — it is a call to every person to reimagine their relationship with water. By choosing conscious daily habits, you become part of a global movement that is reshaping how humanity uses and values this essential resource.

Remember: The UN SDG 6 global target is to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030. The time to act is now — and it starts at home.

🚀 Take Action Now

Ready to make a difference? Here are three achievable commitments you can make as a student or household member today:

  1. Audit your home's water use this week. Read your water meter at the same time on two consecutive days to understand your baseline usage.
  2. Choose three habits from the checklist above and commit to them for 21 days — the time it takes to form a lasting habit. Track your progress using the interactive checklist on this page.
  3. Spread the word. Share this page or the BLUEDROP Action Impact Simulator with one friend or family member. Collective action starts with a single conversation.

Together, these steps connect individual choices to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6, ensuring clean water and sanitation remains a reality — not a privilege — for everyone, everywhere.
