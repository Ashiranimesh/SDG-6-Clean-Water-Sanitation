United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 6

Water is life.
Let's protect it together.

By 2030, over 2 billion people still lack access to safe drinking water. BLUEDROP is a student-led initiative connecting you with real actions to help achieve SDG 6 — right now, from where you are.

Why It Matters

The Water Crisis Is Everyone's Problem

Clean water is not a privilege — it is a right. Here's why SDG 6 matters to you, your community, and the future.

The Problem

2.2 Billion People Lack Safe Water

In many countries, students skip school, women walk hours daily, and children drink contaminated water — directly blocking education, health, and opportunity.

  • Fix dripping taps — one drip wastes 20L/day
  • Advocate for campus refill stations
  • Avoid single-use plastic bottles
Student Action

You Can Drive Real Change

Your choices ripple outward. From conservation habits to policy advocacy, every action counts in the global water equation.

  • Run a water-audit of your accommodation
  • Join campus sustainability committees
  • Share water-saving content on social media
The Vision

A World Where Water Is Universal

If we act collectively, by 2030 every person could have safe water and sanitation — healthier communities, stronger economies, a planet in balance.

  • Healthier populations, fewer preventable deaths
  • Girls able to stay in school, not fetch water
  • Ecosystems restored and biodiversity protected
